Río Texas pastors: your formula is built in.

FairShare uses your conference's actual formula — the two-year average of Lines 41–47 divided by the conference-wide total — not a generic estimate. Same engine for North Georgia, Louisiana, Texas Annual, and Arkansas. Your conference, your numbers, no setup.

We don't suggest reclassifications. We don't help you minimize what you legitimately owe. We show you what your conference's published rules say — every line cited — and let you make honest decisions with clear information.

Three ways to use FairShare

How it works.

Scenario

One-off what-if

Your finance committee is debating a part-time youth director. Type the salary, pick the category, see the apportionment impact in real time. Change the category — watch it move. The classification logic, made visible.

Budget Planner

Running list

Track every approved and proposed line item. A dashboard shows how this year's budget will affect next year's apportionment — and what adding the proposed items would do on top. Built for the monthly finance committee meeting.

Year-End Review

The spreadsheet

When January comes and Table II is due, download the Year-End Review spreadsheet. It applies the same rules engine to your completed expenses and shows whether you're about to overpay — line by line, with citations your treasurer can defend to the conference.
